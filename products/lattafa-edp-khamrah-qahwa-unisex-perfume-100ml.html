If Khamrah is a spiced Arabian evening, Qahwa is that same evening with a coffee in hand — and it might be even better.

Launched in 2023 as a sibling to the original Khamrah, Qahwa opens with cinnamon, cardamom, and ginger — an intensely spiced, almost gingerbread-like opening that's warm and immediate. The heart moves into praline, candied fruits, and white flowers, building a creamy, sweetly complex core. Then the base arrives and everything clicks: coffee, vanilla, tonka bean, benzoin, and musk settle into a rich, deeply gourmand finish that's cosy without ever feeling heavy-handed.

The coffee note is the masterstroke. Rather than dominating, it emerges gradually as the fragrance develops on skin, grounding the sweetness of the praline and spices in something darker and more sophisticated.

Performance is exceptional — longevity of 14–15 hours on skin and days on clothing. A Fragrantica Readers' Choice Award winner for 2024. Best worn in autumn and winter. One or two sprays maximum.

Why We Carry This

We carry Khamrah already, and Qahwa felt like the natural companion — same DNA, different twist. Where the original leans into dates and amber, Qahwa goes darker with the coffee note, and for a lot of people that makes it the better of the two.

The dry-down on this one is genuinely special. The coffee doesn't announce itself immediately — it creeps in as the spices settle, and by the time it arrives the whole thing has become this warm, slightly boozy, spiced coffee gourmand that's incredibly hard to stop smelling on your own wrist.

Performance is absurd for the price. If you're buying Khamrah, honestly consider getting both — they're different enough to earn separate space in any collection.
