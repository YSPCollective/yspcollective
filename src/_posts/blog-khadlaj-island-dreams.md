Summer has a scent. Not sunscreen — not that kind. The kind you reach for when you step out of the shower on a hot morning and want to smell like you've got somewhere worth going. Clean, bright, alive. That's what Khadlaj Island Dreams is.

We've been stocking it as part of our summer series and we wanted to give it the proper write-up it deserves — because this one has a story worth telling.

---

## The Brand Behind It

Khadlaj was founded in the UAE in 1997, a family-owned house built by master perfumer Mohamed Iqbal Abdul Sattar — a man with over 45 years in the craft. The brand's foundation is traditional Arabian perfumery: oud, musk, rose, and the rich blending culture of the Gulf. But Khadlaj has never been content to stay in one lane. Their catalogue spans deep orientals and airy contemporaries, and they do both well.

Island Dreams sits firmly in the contemporary camp. It's Khadlaj stepping out of the souk and into the marina.

## What It Actually Smells Like

Island Dreams is an Extrait de Parfum — a higher concentration than standard EDPs, which means more oil, more depth, and better longevity even from what looks like a simple composition.

**Top:** Bergamot and ginger — a sparkling, zesty opening with just enough spice to stop it feeling flat. The bergamot is bright and Sicilian-style clean; the ginger cuts through with a gentle sharpness rather than a kitchen burn.

**Heart:** Grapefruit accord — this is the engine of the fragrance. A vivid, tart burst of citrus that feels genuinely summery. Not synthetic lemon-floor-cleaner territory. Proper, juicy grapefruit.

**Base:** Ambroxan and musk — the classic contemporary base. Warm, smooth, skin-close. Ambroxan is the note that gives fragrances that "expensive" quality — the feeling that the scent is coming from your skin rather than floating above it.

The overall effect is: clean, crisp, solar, with a musky warmth underneath that keeps it from evaporating off you after 20 minutes. Longevity is strong — 7–8 hours is well within reach, which for a fresh citrus concentration is genuinely impressive.

---

## Who Wears It

Unisex is the official designation, and it holds up. The opening skews slightly masculine — there's an energy to the ginger and bergamot combination that feels direct and confident. The dry-down is softer, more skin-like, more genuinely universal.

It's an all-day warm-weather fragrance. Morning commute, Sunday terrace, beach bag — it fits all of it. The projection is moderate rather than room-filling, which is exactly right for the season.

## The Inspired-By Angle: LV Symphony

<!-- [IMAGE 2: Flat lay or lifestyle shot — bottle alongside summer objects, perhaps on a white surface] -->

Here's the conversation everyone in the fragrance community is having about Island Dreams: it shares its DNA with Louis Vuitton Symphony.

Symphony is part of LV's Les Extraits collection — Jacques Cavallier Belletrud's work — built on the same grapefruit, bergamot, ginger and ambroxan backbone. It's a beautiful fragrance. It's also around €500 for 100ml.

Island Dreams plays in the same world. Citrus brightness up top, ginger warmth at the heart, that ambroxan-musk base doing the long-game work underneath. The character is recognisably similar.

Where Island Dreams distinguishes itself: it's bolder. The ginger is more pronounced, the citrus more vivid, the projection more assertive. Symphony tends to be elegant and restrained, almost whispering. Island Dreams speaks up. For wearing outside in actual summer heat — rather than in an air-conditioned atelier in Paris — that confidence suits the context better.

You're not choosing Island Dreams because you can't afford Symphony. You're choosing it because for daily summer use, it performs just as well, projects better, and costs a fraction. At under €40 for an Extrait de Parfum, the value calculation isn't even close.
